Single Water Connection — How to Share It

Many Munich apartments have exactly one cold-water tap (Waschmaschinenanschluss) for kitchen appliances. Here is how to connect both a washing machine and a dishwasher to it without a plumber.

The problem

A standard German kitchen washing-machine tap (G 3/4" angle valve) outputs one cold-water connection. A dishwasher also requires a G 3/4" cold-water inlet. You cannot plug both hoses into a single valve — you need either a splitter or a manual swap arrangement.

Key constraint

Both appliances should never run simultaneously on a split connection — shared water pressure reduces flow to each machine, which can trigger error codes and poor wash results. A good Y-splitter includes individual shut-off valves so exactly one appliance runs at a time.

Your options

Recommended

Y-Splitter with isolation valves

A T-piece angle-valve splitter (Winkelventil-Verteiler, G 3/4" × 2) screws directly onto the existing tap. Each outlet has its own shut-off lever — open one, close the other. Both appliance hoses stay permanently connected. Takes 10 minutes to install, zero plumbing skill required.

Where to buy: Bauhaus, OBI, Amazon.de — search for Waschmaschine Verteiler 3/4 Doppelventil. Brands: Schell, Sanha, Gardena.

~€15 – €35
Workable

Manual hose swap

Unscrew the active machine's inlet hose from the tap, attach the other machine's hose, and run the programme. Free and requires no additional parts. Practical only if one appliance runs infrequently (e.g. the dishwasher is used weekly). Daily swapping is impractical and wears out the hose connector seal over time.

€0
Avoid

Run both simultaneously without splitter

Both machines drawing from a single valve without isolation causes pressure drops. Most dishwashers will trigger an E1/E3 water intake error within the first fill cycle. Washing machines may show similar errors or simply produce poor wash results due to insufficient water.

Not viable

Installation steps

  1. Turn off the water Close the existing angle valve fully (clockwise). Place a towel under the tap to catch residual water.
  2. Unscrew the existing hose Remove the washing machine inlet hose from the angle valve. Dry the valve thread and wrap 3–4 layers of Teflon tape clockwise around the thread.
  3. Screw on the Y-splitter Hand-tighten the Y-splitter onto the tap, then tighten one more turn with a wrench. Both outlet levers should be in the closed (off) position.
  4. Connect both inlet hoses Screw the washing machine hose onto outlet A and the dishwasher hose onto outlet B. Hand-tighten plus a quarter turn — do not over-tighten nylon-sealed connectors.
  5. Install the dual-inlet siphon Replace the existing drain siphon under the sink with a Doppel-Siphon. Connect the washing machine drain hose to one inlet and the dishwasher drain hose to the other. The dishwasher drain hose must loop up behind the machine (anti-siphon loop) before entering the siphon — follow the appliance manual.
  6. Test for leaks Open the main valve and one outlet lever at a time. Run a short cycle on each machine and check all connections for drips. The dual-inlet siphon should be checked during the drain phase of each machine.
Reminder — one at a time

Stick a note on the splitter or the machines: always check that the other lever is closed before starting a programme. This is the only operational change from having two separate connections.

Research notes
Price accuracy

Prices verified at mediamarkt.de on 28 June 2026 and otto.de in July 2026. Prices include German VAT and may change. Promotions not factored in.

Place settings vs. household size

For 2 people: 14 place settings is comfortably sized — you can run every 2–3 days without overflowing. A full cycle costs roughly €0.60–0.80 in electricity and water in Munich (including heating).

Noise levels

Under 44 dB: inaudible from the next room. 46–50 dB: faint hum — fine in a closed kitchen. 50+ dB: noticeable while watching TV in an open plan space. Tier 3+ machines all stay under 44 dB.

60 cm vs. 45 cm (slimline)

Slimline models (~45 cm wide, 9–10 place settings) are only worth it if you have genuine space constraints. For a 2-person household, a full 60 cm machine running every 3 days uses less water/energy than a slimline running daily.

Zeolite drying (Tier 3+)

Zeolite drying absorbs moisture and releases it as heat — dishes emerge completely dry, no towel needed, no rinse-aid smell. Standard on Siemens iQ300/500 and some AEG/Bosch models. The mineral lasts the machine's lifetime.

Munich water hardness

Munich tap water is very hard (14–15° dH). Always fill the rinse-aid and salt dispensers — even if the machine says "no salt required". Hard water leaves white deposits on glasses without regular salt. Use Calgon or equivalent every 2–3 months.

Research notes
Self-empty vs. self-wash vs. self-dry docks

Budget models charge only. Mid-tier docks add auto-emptying dust bags and mop washing. Premium docks add hot-air or hot-water drying so the mop doesn't smell musty between cleans — worth it if the robot runs daily.

LiDAR vs. camera navigation

LiDAR (LDS) maps rooms reliably in the dark and is the budget-tier default. AI camera hybrids (mid/premium tiers) add object recognition — useful for avoiding cables, shoes, and pet mess, but need reasonable ambient light.

Carpets and thresholds

Check auto mop-lift is supported before buying if the living room has any rugs — otherwise the robot wets carpet edges. Higher-tier models (Saros 20) can also climb taller door thresholds without getting stuck.

Not a moving-in priority

Unlike the other living-room items, this is purely a convenience purchase — buy once the essentials (desk, lamp, sofa) are sorted and only if daily light cleaning is genuinely wanted.

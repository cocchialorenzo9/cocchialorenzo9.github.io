Notes & Context

What is Grundversorgung?

The statutory fallback that every German grid operator must provide. In Munich, SWM is the designated Grundversorger. If you move in and do nothing, you land here automatically. It's reliable — just overpriced. You can exit with only 2 weeks' notice and no cancellation fee.

How to switch providers

Sign up online with any new provider — they handle the switch paperwork with SWM and your grid operator automatically. The process takes 2–4 weeks; you don't need to contact SWM yourself. For competitive contracts, check the Kündigungsfrist (usually 6 weeks before the Mindestlaufzeit ends) to avoid auto-renewal into a new term.

Ökostrom certification hierarchy

1. Grüner Strom Label & ok-power (best): require direct investment in new German renewable plants — Naturstrom uses both. 2. TÜV SÜD/Nord: certifies renewable origin but no new-plant investment required — most providers here. 3. Self-declared "100% renewable": no third-party audit, weakest claim. Only labels 1 and 2 are independently verified.

Tibber & dynamic pricing

Prices update every hour based on the European Day-Ahead market. You need a smart meter (Intelligentes Messsystem / iMSys) — ask your landlord if one is installed. Without one, Tibber temporarily puts you on a provisional fixed rate. Smart meters are mandatory for new builds and properties drawing ≥10 kW, but many older Munich flats don't have one yet.

Ostrom's monthly rate can change

The SimplyFair (monthly) rate of 22.62 ct/kWh reflects June 2026 market conditions. Ostrom adjusts this monthly — it was higher in 2024/2025 and can rise in winter when spot prices increase. Their SimplyFair12 locks in a rate for 12 months at a higher per-kWh price; use it if you want predictability.

Hidden caveats to watch

New-customer bonuses (Neukundenbonus) are often paid only after 12 months — cancel early and you forfeit them. Check whether the price guarantee covers taxes and levies (Steuern & Abgaben) or only the energy component; most guarantees cover energy only, so surcharges can still rise. Always compare with your exact Munich postal code on Check24 or Verivox — prices vary by network zone.
